objective: The study aimed to characterize lesions of coccidioidomycosis in South American camelids and assess its importance as a cause of pneumonia in alpacas and llamas.
background: Coccidioidomycosis is a mycosis caused by Coccidioides immitis or Coccidioides posadasii. Scientific literature on South American camelids is scarce, but anecdotal evidence suggests a particular susceptibility of these species to respiratory disease caused by Coccidioides spp.
methods: The authors examined the autopsy records of the California Animal Health and Food Safety system from 1992 to 2013. Archived histological samples from 42 cases were reassessed, and in some formalin-fixed, paraffin-embedded tissue samples, PCR was performed to identify and distinguish Coccidioides spp.

  patient: South American camelids subjected to autopsy in the CAHFS system between 1992 and 2013; the study included a total of 2,101 animals, with 1,018 llamas and 1,083 alpacas

  surgical: NOT APPLICABLE
  nerve_block: Retrospective review of autopsy and histological findings; molecular identification of Coccidioides spp. by real-time quantitative PCR on paraffin-embedded tissues.
  monitoring: NOT APPLICABLE
findings:
  - Out of 2,101 camelids subjected to autopsy, 362 cases of pneumonia were classified as the cause of death.
  - Coccidioides spp. was responsible for 66 pneumonias out of 362, accounting for 18% of cases.
  - Coccidioidomycosis was more often disseminated than limited to the lung.
  - Macroscopic lesions were multifocal or confluent, yellow-white, and pyogranulomatous.
  - Both C. immitis and C. posadasii were identified in PCR-positive samples.
clinical_implications:
  - Coccidioidomycosis should be considered an important differential diagnosis in pneumonia in alpacas and llamas.
  - The disease can involve multiple organs, so it should not be interpreted as a simple localized respiratory pathology.
  - Histological diagnosis alone does not allow for certain differentiation of the Coccidioides species involved.
  - Suspect samples require biosafety precautions, as handling may expose personnel to infectious risk.
limitations:
  - Retrospective study based on autopsy reports collected over 21 years.
  - Possible underestimation of small pulmonary foci not detected or reported in the records.
  - Not all samples were available for molecular analysis.
  - The work reflects cases primarily from California, so geographic generalizability is limited.
take_home:
  - Coccidioidomycosis is a relevant cause of pneumonia in South American camelids.
  - In the cases studied, the disseminated form was more common than the purely pulmonary form.
  - Coccidioides spp. can affect both llamas and alpacas, with similar lesions in both species.
  - Species confirmation requires molecular methods, not histology alone.

Coccidioidomycosis represents an important differential diagnosis in pneumonia cases in alpacas and llamas, especially in endemic areas. The study is relevant because it shows that infection can be frequent in autopsy cases and often presents in disseminated form, thus involving systemic rather than solely respiratory involvement. Methodologically, the work is robust due to the breadth of the case series and the combined use of histology and molecular confirmation, but it remains limited by its retrospective nature and geographically restricted origin. In clinical practice, the most useful message is that in the presence of chronic respiratory signs or nodular lung lesions in camelids, coccidioidomycosis should be considered early and managed carefully, also due to the zoonotic risk associated with tissue handling.
