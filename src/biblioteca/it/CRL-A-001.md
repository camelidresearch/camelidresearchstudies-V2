This article represents one of the few reports of peripheral regional anaesthesia applied to a South American camelid species. The detailed description of the RUMM nerve block technique, including landmarks, electrical parameters and drug doses, provides a valuable reference for clinicians managing orthopaedic conditions in alpacas.

While the evidence level is limited to a single case, the clinical outcome was encouraging and suggests that regional anaesthesia techniques deserve further investigation in camelid practice. The use of electrostimulation guidance adds reproducibility and may facilitate adoption in field or hospital settings.

Camelid Research Studies APS considers this article a useful educational resource for veterinarians approaching regional anaesthesia in New World camelids.
