Camelid Research Studies APS officially opens to **collaborations** with professionals and institutions that share our goals of promoting the health and well-being of domestic South American camelids.

We believe that scientific research grows through the exchange of expertise and the building of networks. For this reason, we invite anyone who wishes to contribute, in various ways, to the study and care of alpacas and llamas to get in touch with us.

## Who we want to collaborate with

The association is open to dialogue with **veterinarians** interested in camelid medicine, with **universities and research centers** that wish to develop shared projects, with **breeders** willing to contribute clinical, behavioral, and management data, and with **associations, public and private entities** that promote animal health and well-being.

## Areas of collaboration

Collaboration opportunities cover all our areas of activity: from the collection of clinical and epidemiological data to the study of welfare and stress indicators, from the development of protocols for animal-assisted interventions to training and scientific dissemination.

Among our first projects for 2026–2027 are the study on stress and welfare assessment in alpacas and llamas involved in assisted activities, the creation of a database on camelid health, and the development of a dedicated research network. Each of these projects can grow thanks to the contribution of new partners.

## How to contact us

Those interested in collaborating can write to us through the **Contacts** page on the website. We will be happy to evaluate together possible forms of collaboration and to build common paths in the service of research and the well-being of camelids.
