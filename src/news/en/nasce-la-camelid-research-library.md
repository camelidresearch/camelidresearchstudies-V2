We are pleased to announce the launch of the **Camelid Research Library**, the scientific library of Camelid Research Studies APS dedicated to literature on domestic South American camelids.

The library collects **summary sheets** of peer-reviewed scientific articles on alpacas and llamas: each sheet clearly and structurally summarizes the objectives, methods, results, and clinical implications of a published study. The goal is to make scientific research more accessible to veterinarians, breeders, students, and industry professionals.

## What each sheet contains

Each sheet in the library includes the essential data of the original article — authors, journal, year, verified DOI — along with a summary of key points: study objective, context, methodology, main results, clinical implications, and limitations. Where relevant, the protocol used is also described.

To this information is added an **association commentary**: a critical evaluation that frames the relevance and usefulness of the study for camelid medicine and welfare. This commentary is prepared by our scientific team and clearly distinguishes the facts reported in the article from the association's interpretation.

## A growing project

The library starts with a few curated sheets and will gradually expand. We have chosen to prioritize **quality over quantity**: each sheet is verified and reviewed before publication to ensure scientific accuracy and fidelity to sources.

The sheets are available in the **Resources** section of the website. Each sheet can be saved as a PDF and includes a QR code linking to the online page, making it easier to share among professionals.

We will continue to expand the library in the coming months, alongside the launch of our 2026–2027 research projects.
